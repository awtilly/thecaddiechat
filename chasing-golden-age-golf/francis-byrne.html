Francis A. Byrne
Golf Course

A public Charles Banks gem loaded with Golden Age template holes — the Biarritz, the Road Hole, the Eden — all for under $60.

ArchitectCharles Banks (Seth Raynor plans)
Year1926
LocationWest Orange, NJ
AccessPublic
RenovationStephen Kay, 2023

The Story

Francis Byrne is one of the best-kept secrets in public golf. Seth Raynor designed it in 1925 as the West Course for the adjacent Essex County Country Club, but he died before construction began. His protégé Charles Banks completed the build — making this course part of a direct architectural lineage from Charles Blair Macdonald, the father of American golf course design, through Raynor, to Banks.

After WWII, financial difficulties at ECCC forced the West Course open to the public. Essex County purchased it outright in 1978 and renamed it after Francis A. Byrne, a local town commissioner whose son Brendan would go on to become Governor of New Jersey.

Architecture & Design

The course sits on a dramatically hilly property, built into the side of a substantial ridge. What makes it special are the template holes — designs that Macdonald deemed "ideal" and that Raynor and Banks adapted across dozens of courses. Rarely are these templates accessible to public golfers. Here, they are:

Road Hole (St. Andrews 17th)
Hole 2

Hole 5
Short template
Hole 14
Eden template

Stephen Kay's 2023 renovation restored many features that had been softened over decades — Banks's bold green contours and bunkering came back to life, and new fescue plantings add both beauty and penalty. The course rates a punishing 74.1 / 140 slope from the Blue Tees.

Part of Essex County's underrated municipal collection alongside Rock Spring (Raynor), Hendricks Field (Banks), and Weequahic (George Low) — some of the strongest public Golden Age architecture in America.

What Stayed With Us

Walking off the 2nd green — a proper Biarritz that can stretch to 265 yards — you start to realize what you're standing on. This isn't some average muni. These are the same template designs that grace the most exclusive clubs in the country, and here they are for $42 with a county pass. The elevation changes will test your legs, but the architecture will test your imagination. That's the real gift of this place.
