The Founding

Winged Foot Golf Club is one of the most revered golf courses in the world. Designed by Albert Warren Tillinghast and opened in 1923, the West Course has hosted six U.S. Opens and is consistently ranked among the top courses in America.

Tillinghast was given a simple but ambitious directive: build a championship course worthy of the nation's best players. He delivered beyond expectations, creating both the West and East courses.

Architectural Significance

The West Course is Tillinghast's magnum opus. Everything that defined his philosophy is here: heroic bunkering, green complexes that demand precision from every angle, and a routing that builds in drama as the round progresses.

Tillinghast's bunkering at Winged Foot is legendary. Deep, steep-faced bunkers guard every approach, creating visual intimidation and real strategic consequences. The greens are among the most challenging in championship golf — heavily contoured, sloping, and deceptively fast.

On the Ground

Walking Winged Foot was a pilgrimage. Every hole carries the weight of championship history. The course is every bit as intimidating as its reputation. The bunkering is relentless, the greens are severe, and the overall difficulty is sustained from the first tee to the final putt. But it's also beautiful — simultaneously terrifying and gorgeous.
