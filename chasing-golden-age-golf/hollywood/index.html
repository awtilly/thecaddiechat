The Founding

Hollywood Golf Club in Deal, New Jersey, is another jewel in Walter Travis's architectural crown. Opened in 1917, the course sits on dramatic terrain along the Jersey Shore, where the rolling hills and ocean breezes create playing conditions that change with every wind shift.

Architectural Significance

Hollywood represents Travis at his most dramatic. The terrain here is more pronounced — bigger elevation changes, more dramatic vistas, and a sense of being perched above the landscape. Travis used this terrain masterfully with tee shots over valleys and along ridgelines.

The course has maintained much of its original character over more than a century, a testament to the strength of Travis's original routing.

On the Ground

Hollywood was, in a word, unforgettable. The combination of dramatic terrain, demanding shot values, and the ever-present wind created a golf experience unlike anything else on our trip. Standing on the elevated tees, looking out over the undulating terrain, you understand why people fall in love with Golden Age golf.
