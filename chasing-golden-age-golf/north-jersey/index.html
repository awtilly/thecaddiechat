The Founding

North Jersey Country Club, designed by Walter Travis and opened in 1923, represents one of the finest examples of a player-turned-architect's vision. Travis, who won three U.S. Amateurs and a British Amateur, brought a competitor's eye to course design that few architects could match.

Architectural Significance

Travis's approach was shaped by his experience as a player. He understood what made holes challenging, fair, and memorable. His designs at North Jersey reflect this — strategic test after strategic test, where the thinking golfer is rewarded.

Travis was known for his emphasis on the short game, and North Jersey's green complexes reflect this priority with subtle contours that demand precise approach shots.

On the Ground

Playing North Jersey, you can feel the competitor's hand in the design. Every hole presents a clear challenge and a clear reward for the correct play. The course has a compactness that belies its challenge — it uses angles, contours, and strategic bunkering to create a puzzle that demands both physical skill and mental acuity.
