The Founding

Essex County Country Club holds the distinction of being one of the oldest country clubs in New Jersey, founded in 1887. Over its remarkable history, the course has been shaped by multiple architectural hands — including A.W. Tillinghast, Seth Raynor, and Charles Banks.

Thomas Edison was among its early members, and the club has hosted numerous significant events over its more than 130-year history.

Architectural Significance

What makes Essex County architecturally fascinating is the layered influence of multiple Golden Age masters. Tillinghast's strategic vision, Raynor's template hole concepts, and Banks's construction expertise all left their mark. The result is a design that represents a collaborative masterpiece.

On the Ground

Playing Essex County felt like walking through a living museum of Golden Age golf. Every hole carried the weight of history, yet the course played as fresh and challenging as anything built in the modern era. The blend of architectural influences created a variety that kept us guessing from start to finish.
